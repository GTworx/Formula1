### Career Profile & Biography
Born in London but racing under the flag of Thailand, Alexander Albon’s first word was in fact Italian. That word was Ferrari – though it was with another Italian team that he got his big F1 break. Idolising Michael Schumacher, Albon was pipped to the 2016 GP3 title by Charles Leclerc and took the 2018 Formula 2 title fight down to the wire alongside George Russell and Lando Norris. Promoted mid-season to Red Bull Racing in 2019, he scored top-six finishes in eight of his nine 2019 Red Bull races. After serving as Red Bull reserve driver in 2021, he returned to the grid with Williams in 2022 where he established a reputation as a fast qualifier and mature team leader.
